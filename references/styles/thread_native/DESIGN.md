# Style: Thread Native

**For:** Type-forward carousels that read like a screenshotted Twitter thread
or notes-app slide. Less designed, more native.

**Mood:** casual, immediate, conversational. The viewer should feel like they
are reading a thread, not consuming a designed asset.

## Do
- Type-forward composition: text dominates, no decoration
- Tight line height, comfortable left margin
- One thought per slide, broken naturally across the carousel
- Body weight throughout; reserve semibold for the hook
- White or near-white background; black or near-black text

## Don't
- Brand palettes or accent colors (this is the "no brand" look)
- Hero images, illustrations, or 3D renders
- Pill tags, badges, or any UI chrome that screams "designed"
- Gradients, drop shadows, textures
- Typography flourishes (this is system-ui or close)

## When to swap to a different style
If the post needs character/persona presence or visual identity, switch to
the brand's character-driven style. This style is for posts where the
message is the design and no person appears.

## Compatible with

All seven formats: narrative, numbered_diagnostic, receipt_context,
process_reveal, anatomy_breakdown, before_after, counter_narrative.

The visual style is orthogonal to the structural format. Pick a format based
on the story's natural shape; pick a style based on the brand's visual system.
