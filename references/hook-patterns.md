# Hook patterns for carousel scripts

Six patterns the concept-generator skill uses to produce hook variety. Each
hook variant in a concept gets tagged with one of these names so the operator
can compare across patterns.

## single_claim

One emotional pull, one complete idea, demands the next line.

**When to use:** tactical receipts, dollar amounts, replacement-of-X claims.

**Exemplars:**
- "i replaced my $11k product photographer with one prompt."
- "gpt image 2 just killed my $11k product photographer."
- "i run my meta ads with @openclaw for $0/month."

## question

Curiosity gap that demands resolution.

**When to use:** ICP education, framework introduction, contrarian setup.

**Exemplars:**
- "what does claude know about your brand that you don't?"
- "why does the same model produce $11k output for one operator and garbage for another?"

## scene

Dated, sensory, specific opening moment.

**When to use:** personal anecdote, transformation, before/after story.

**Exemplars:**
- "tuesday 2pm. the agency invoice line was gone."
- "10 minutes scrolling. 12 carousels. 4 of them used identical structure."

## dialogue

Quoted line that becomes the load-bearing element of the post.

**When to use:** receipt + context, counter-narrative, borrowed authority.

**Exemplars:**
- "a $15M ARR founder told me: 'ai carousels all look the same.'"
- "lemkin said it on his last podcast: 'the agency model is dead.'"

## contrast

Two things that should not both be true; paradox surface.

**When to use:** process reveal, before/after, anatomy breakdowns.

**Exemplars:**
- "we doubled output. we cut headcount in half."
- "the model that designs them is invisible. the carousels it designs are obvious."

## observation

Insider point at something the reader will see in their own world.

**When to use:** numbered diagnostic, counter-narrative, ICP education.

**Exemplars:**
- "scroll your feed. count the carousels with exactly 6 slides."
- "most founders are still hiring marketing teams when they could be building agents."

## 360 shock-score

The skill rates each hook 0-10 across six axes:

- emotional_pull (does it stop a scroll)
- specificity (concrete numbers, dates, names)
- tension (a problem, a stake, a cost)
- named (named person, brand, or framework)
- dated (explicit time anchor)
- contrarian (cuts against orthodoxy)

Aggregate score is the sum (0-60). Scores surface in `concepts.md` so the
operator can pick by axis priority, not just total.
